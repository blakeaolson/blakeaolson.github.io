Recently, a paradigm of diffusion language models has grown popular due to theoretical speed-ups in inference and a lack of reliance on autoregressive models (ARM). ARMs work by sequentially predicting tokens. It is modeled as a conditional probability of predicting the current word given all previous words.

Intuitively, humans do not think word by word. We have different thoughts that collide and often take shape after multiple iterations. This is why diffusion makes sense: We do not have to only condition our output based on previous words. We can provide an output based on the entire sequence and iteratively refine it.

This paper hopes to remove reliance on ARMs completely. The fundamental backbone is still a transformer, but it relies on bi-directional attention with a masked language model objective (See BERT). The goal is not to generate token by token, but to instead iteratively demask words from a fixed-length sequence. The model eventually demasks all tokens to reveal an answer
